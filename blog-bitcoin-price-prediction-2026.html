Halving Cycle Analysis

Bitcoin has undergone four halvings (2012, 2016, 2020, 2024). Each halving cuts the block reward in half, reducing new supply by 50%. Historically, the price peak occurs 12-18 months after the halving.

Key Price Factors to Watch

Bullish Factors

  • Supply dynamics: Only 21 million BTC will ever exist, with ~19.8 million already mined

  • Macro recession: A severe global recession could trigger sell-offs across all risk assets
  • Technology risk: Quantum computing threats (though likely 10+ years away from being relevant)
  • Competition: Other crypto assets or CBDCs could capture market share

Frequently Asked Questions

Will Bitcoin hit $200,000 in 2026?

Based on halving cycle analysis and current ETF demand, $200,000 is within the base case scenario for 2026. However, timing and magnitude of cycle peaks vary.
